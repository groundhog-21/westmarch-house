# Westmarch House — User Guide
A Practical Handbook for Patrons of the Estate

## 1. Introduction
This guide describes how to operate the Westmarch House multi-agent system through its Streamlit interface. It provides a functional overview of each service, accompanied by example prompts and usage guidance.

---

## 2. Using the Interface

### 2.1 Mode Selection
The main sidebar presents the available services:

- Parlour Discussions  
- Arrangements for the Day  
- Matters Requiring Investigation  
- Correspondence & Drafting  
- Records & Summaries from the Archive  
- Her Ladyship’s Critique  
- Matters Requiring the Whole Household  
- Jeeves Remembers  
- Mystery in the Archives (Surprise Demonstration)  

Choose a mode, then supply relevant instructions.

---

## 3. Service Descriptions

### 3.1 Parlour Discussions (General Conversation)
General discussion with Jeeves.  
Useful for small talk, gentle queries, or casual interaction.

### 3.2 Arrangements for the Day
Daily planning.  
Produces structured schedules with optional refinements.

### 3.3 Matters Requiring Investigation
Research tasks handled by Perkins.  
Examples:
- “Compare ETFs and mutual funds.”  
- “Summarize the properties of the wren.”

### 3.4 Correspondence & Drafting
Miss Pennington drafts emails, letters, or polished prose.

### 3.5 Records & Summaries from the Archive
Jeeves retrieves summaries or context from earlier session notes.

### 3.6 Her Ladyship's Critique (Proceed with Caution)
Submit a poem or paragraph for incisive evaluation.

### 3.7 Matters Requiring the Whole Household
All agents collaborate on a non-linear task.  
The demo case: *The Misbehaving Garden Gnome*.

### 3.8 Jeeves Remembers
Queries past actions or events stored in memory.  
Uses advanced memory scoring and domain inference.

### 3.9 Mystery in the Archives
A bonus demonstration: a hidden case discovered in the estate’s records.  
Shows archival queries, multi-agent deduction, and stylistic coordination.

---

## 4. Interaction Guidelines

### 4.1 Tone
The system responds best to genteel phrasing, though it accepts natural requests.

### 4.2 Closure Detection
You may conclude interactions with:
- “Thank you, Jeeves.”  
- “That will be all.”  
- “Much obliged.”  

Jeeves will gracefully retire.

### 4.3 Memory Queries
Examples:
- “What did I tell you yesterday about my poem?”  
- “Did we ever discuss investing?”  
- “Was there something about a gnome moving in the garden?”

---

## 5. Troubleshooting

### 5.1 No Relevant Memory
Jeeves will respond with an elegant fallback and may request additional clues.

### 5.2 Ambiguous Queries
You may refine by specifying:
- a topic  
- an approximate date  
- an agent involved  
- or domain (e.g., finance, poetry)

## 6. Conclusion
This guide equips patrons with the necessary knowledge to engage comfortably and effectively with Westmarch House and its distinguished staff.
